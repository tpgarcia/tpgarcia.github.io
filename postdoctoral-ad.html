Postdoctoral Researcher

This position will provide a unique opportunity to be part of an enthusiastic research team working towards predicting precise, personalized, and robust progression profiles for neurodegenerative diseases. Interested candidates must send (1) Curriculum Vitae/Resume and (2) List of 3-4 references (contact information) to tpgarcia[at]stat[dot]tamu[dot]edu. Due to federally-mandated funded restrictions, the postdoctoral trainee MUST be a US citizen or permanent resident.

Who We Are

Our research goal is to design robust, reliable, and parsimonious disease progression models for Alzheimer's, Parkinson's and Huntington disease. We build statistical models using longitudinal imaging data and clinical data about motor, cognitive, functional impairments to predict when significant impairments are likely to occur. Our group has developed semiparametric/nonparametric models to develop longitudinal prediction models that identify patient subgroups susceptible to faster motor impairment, cognitive impairment, and regional brain atophy. We also developed variable selection methods to extract maximal information from large, highly correlated imaging data to identify brain regions that could be target regions for future clinical trials. See our list of our publications here.

Active projects in the Garcia Research Group are focused on addressing the following questions:

  1. how can we identify patient subgroups with distinct cognitive decline and regional brain atrophy when data is collected from individuals with different disease severity levels but without clear markers for their exact severity level?
  2. how can we predict an individual's expected disease progression pattern when there is substantial missing data?
  3. how can we predict significant cognitive decline from high-dimensional imaging data and develop robust inferential tools?
