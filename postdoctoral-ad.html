Who We Are:

Our research goal is to design robust, reliable, and parsimonious disease progression models for Huntington and Alzheimer disease. We build statistical models using longitudinal data about motor, cognitive, functional impairments to predict when significant impairments are likely to occur. Our group has developed semiparametric/nonparametric models to develop longitudinal prediction models that are robust to modeling errors. We also developed variable selection methods to extract maximal information from large, highly correlated imaging data to identify brain regions that could be target regions for future clinical trials. See our list of our publications here.

Active projects in the Garcia Research Group are focused on addressing the following questions:

  1. how can we identify patient subgroups with distinct cognitive decline and regional brain atrophy when data is collected from individuals with different disease severity levels but without clear markers for their exact severity level?
  2. how can we predict an individual's expected disease progression pattern when there is substantial missing data?
  3. how can we predict significant cognitive decline from high-dimensional imaging data and develop robust inferential tools?
