Postdoctoral Researcher

The Garcia Research Group is currently recruiting a highly motivated and driven Postdoctoral researcher with a fresh PhD in Statistics/Biostatistics to join our team at the Department of Biostatistics at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. This position will provide a unique opportunity to be part of an enthusiastic research team working towards extracting maximal information from large, highly correlated data structure to promote scientific discoveries in neurodegenerative diseases. The successful candidate will be supervised by Professor Tanya Garcia and Professor Haibo Zhou.

Who You Are

The ideal candidate is someone who is
  1. Enthusiastic about learning new statistical techniques that can be applied to solve real-world problems.
  2. Highly motivated to pursue and establish a career in research.
  3. Inspired to make a difference in the field.
  4. Highly enthusiastic to seek answers and explanations.
  5. Self-motivated and driven.
  6. Able and willing to work independently.
  7. A fast learner.
  8. Results-driven/outcome oriented.
  9. Flexible with your time.
  10. A critical thinker.
  11. Organized.
  12. Maintains high standards in research.
  13. An excellent communicator.
  14. Open to learning about yourself and your strengths and weaknesses.
  15. Ready to hear about and overcome any patterns that interfere with your productivity and growth as a person and scientist.

Values in the Garcia Research Group:

This research group values creativity, personal fulfillment, integrity, trust, and fun.

Who should not apply:

The position is not a good fit for someone who:
  1. Takes 3+ days to respond to an email.
  2. Begins projects at the last minute.
  3. Prefers thinking over doing.
  4. Does not like improving oneself.
  5. Needs constant motivation and guidance.
  6. Not receptive to constructive criticism.
  7. Looking for an easy research project -- developing high quality research takes time and patience.
  8. Not detail-oriented.
  9. Does not take responsibility for their actions and progress.
  10. Does not want to pursue a career in research.

Job Description and Expectations:

  1. Candidate is expected to critically think, develop, and test statistical methods/models and interpret results independently (this is a MUST).
  2. Implement, test and document statistical methods in R or C++ or Fortran (this is a MUST).
  3. Candidate should have experience in independently writing manuscripts for publication in statistics journals.
  4. Candidate can communicate statistical methods and results in clear and simple language.
  5. Able to work independently after initial training with minimal supervision.
  6. Able to write manuscripts and present at scientific conferences.
  7. We are looking for a candidate who enjoys science and loves solving puzzles, someone who loves the personal growth opportunities in this job.
